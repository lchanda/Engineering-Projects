Popular Spots to Visit

Camelback Mountain

Camelback Mountain is one of Phoenix's most iconic landmarks, standing at 2,704 feet above sea level. It’s named for its unique shape, resembling the hump and head of a camel, and offers some of the best hiking and outdoor experiences in the city. Located in the heart of Phoenix, it attracts hikers, outdoor enthusiasts, and nature lovers looking for a challenging workout with breathtaking views. The mountain features two main hiking trails: the Echo Canyon Trail and the Cholla Trail. Both trails lead to the summit, but Echo Canyon is the more difficult and steep of the two. The summit provides a panoramic view of the city of Phoenix, the surrounding desert landscape, and the distant mountain ranges. On a clear day, visitors can see for miles, making it a popular spot for both sunrise and sunset hikes. Camelback Mountain is not just a popular hiking destination but also a place where nature meets urban life, as it’s surrounded by luxury resorts, golf courses, and high-end shopping areas. Whether you’re an avid hiker or just looking for a scenic spot to relax and take in the beauty of the Sonoran Desert, Camelback Mountain offers an unforgettable experience. It’s a must-visit for anyone exploring Phoenix.

Address: 4925 E McDonald Dr, Phoenix, AZ 85018

Desert Botanical Garden

The Desert Botanical Garden in Phoenix is a stunning 140-acre attraction showcasing the beauty and diversity of desert plant life. Nestled in the heart of Papago Park, this garden features over 50,000 plants, with a focus on desert flora from around the world. Visitors can explore various themed gardens, including the desert wildflower garden, cactus garden, and the agave garden, all of which highlight the adaptability and resilience of desert plants. The garden offers educational exhibits and seasonal displays, including beautiful wildflower blooms in the spring. One of its most remarkable features is its collection of rare and endangered desert species, as well as plants that are native to the arid regions of North and South America. The Desert Botanical Garden is also home to several art installations, interactive exhibits, and frequent events such as live music performances and educational workshops. Its walking trails wind through the desert landscape, providing opportunities to learn about the ecosystems and the vital role these plants play in desert environments. For nature lovers, photographers, and those interested in sustainable practices, the Desert Botanical Garden provides a serene and inspiring environment to connect with the natural world, making it a must-visit spot for anyone in the Phoenix area.

Address: 1201 N Galvin Pkwy, Phoenix, AZ 85008

Roosevelt Row

Roosevelt Row, or "RoRo," is a lively arts district in downtown Phoenix known for its vibrant street art, galleries, and local culture. The area features colorful murals, eclectic shops, and art spaces like The Monorchid and Bentley Gallery. It's also home to popular restaurants like Matt's Big Breakfast and Fez, making it a great spot for both art lovers and foodies. The district comes alive during the monthly First Friday Art Walk, where visitors can explore galleries, enjoy live music, and discover new artists. Roosevelt Row is a creative hub that captures the essence of Phoenix's artistic spirit.

Address: Roosevelt St, Phoenix, AZ 85004
