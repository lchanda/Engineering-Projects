Popular Spots to Visit

Chapel of the Holy Cross

The Chapel of the Holy Cross in Sedona, Arizona, is a stunning Roman Catholic chapel built directly into the red rock cliffs, blending modernist architecture with natural beauty. Completed in 1956 and inspired by the Empire State Building, it was commissioned by sculptor Marguerite Brunswig Staude. The chapel features a dramatic 90-foot iron cross that anchors its design, large glass windows offering panoramic views, and a minimalist interior that fosters peace and reflection. It accommodates around 150 visitors and offers spiritual services, including Mass and quiet meditation. As one of Arizona’s Seven Man-Made Wonders, it’s both a place of worship and a popular landmark for visitors seeking awe and serenity.

Address:780 Chapel Rd, Sedona, AZ 86336

Amitabha Stupa and Peace Park

The Amitabha Stupa and Peace Park in Sedona, Arizona, is a 14-acre spiritual sanctuary nestled at the foot of Thunder Mountain, offering a tranquil space for prayer, meditation, and reflection. Built in 2004 by the Buddhist organization Kunzang Palyul Chöling, the park features a striking 36-foot-tall stupa dedicated to Amitabha, the Buddha of Limitless Light. Surrounded by colorful prayer flags and the majestic red rock landscape, the site welcomes people of all faiths seeking peace, healing, and a deeper spiritual connection. It serves as a powerful symbol of compassion and enlightenment in one of Sedona’s most serene and sacred settings.

Address: 2650 Pueblo Dr, Sedona, AZ 86336

Slide Rock State Park

Slide Rock State Park, situated in Oak Creek Canyon approximately 7 miles north of Sedona, Arizona, is a 43-acre natural haven renowned for its unique sandstone water slide formed by the smooth, algae-covered rocks of Oak Creek. Originally the Pendley Homestead, established in 1910, the area still features a working apple orchard and historic cabins, offering a glimpse into early 20th-century agricultural life. Visitors can enjoy swimming, picnicking, hiking along three scenic trails, and fishing for trout in the creek. Surrounded by towering red rock formations and lush vegetation, the park provides a picturesque setting for outdoor recreation and relaxation.

Address: 6871 AZ-89A, Sedona, AZ 86336
